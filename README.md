## Glossary (All terms in Concept Index)

Quick, plain-language definitions for every term used in the Concept Index. These are study notes, not official definitions.

- **Anapana (breath awareness)**: Awareness of the natural breath, used to steady and sharpen the mind and build concentration (samadhi).
  - First seen: [Day 1 3:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cz7QHNvNFfA&t=187s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-anapana)
- **Anatta (non-self)**: The insight that there is no permanent, independent self behind experience; phenomena arise due to causes and conditions.
  - First seen: [Day 3 54:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXXnSK2a47w&t=3268s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-anatta)
- **Anicca (impermanence)**: Impermanence: everything experienced is constantly changing, arising and passing away.
  - First seen: [Day 3 33:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXXnSK2a47w&t=1989s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-anicca)
- **Aversion**: Pushing away the unpleasant: dislike, irritation, hatred. In practice, it shows up as reactive resistance to sensations.
  - First seen: [Day 1 12:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cz7QHNvNFfA&t=761s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-aversion)
- **Bhanga / free flow / dissolution**: A phase/experience where the apparent solidity of the body breaks into subtle sensations and vibrations, felt as a flowing field.
  - First seen: [Day 3 41:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXXnSK2a47w&t=2465s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-bhanga-free-flow-dissolution)
- **Craving (tanha)**: Wanting and clinging: the pull toward pleasant sensations and experiences. In practice, it is reactive grasping.
  - First seen: [Day 1 32:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cz7QHNvNFfA&t=1931s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-craving)
- **Dhamma (law of nature)**: The law of nature or truth as experienced directly. In these talks, it often means the liberating path of practice and understanding.
  - First seen: [Day 3 6:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXXnSK2a47w&t=419s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-dhamma)
- **Dukkha (suffering)**: Suffering or unsatisfactoriness: the unease that comes with clinging and misunderstanding reality.
  - First seen: [Day 1 13:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cz7QHNvNFfA&t=826s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-dukkha)
- **Equanimity (upekkha)**: A balanced mind that observes sensations without craving or aversion. Central to Vipassana progress.
  - First seen: [Day 3 1:02:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXXnSK2a47w&t=3761s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-equanimity)
- **Nibbana / Nirvana**: Liberation: the cessation of craving and the end of suffering, described as the ultimate goal.
  - First seen: [Day 6 28:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yxp0mZeK2zk&t=1734s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-nibbana-nirvana)
- **Noble Eightfold Path**: The practical framework of training: wisdom (right view/intention), morality (right speech/action/livelihood), and concentration (right effort/mindfulness/concentration).
  - First seen: [Day 2 39:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cYG5VvHry7c&t=2381s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-noble-eightfold-path)
- **Panna (wisdom)**: Experiential wisdom or insight gained through direct observation, not merely intellectual understanding.
  - First seen: [Day 2 29:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cYG5VvHry7c&t=1785s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-panna)
- **Samadhi (concentration)**: Stability and unification of mind. Built first (often through Anapana) so insight practice can go deeper.
  - First seen: [Day 1 11:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cz7QHNvNFfA&t=674s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-samadhi)
- **Sankhara (mental formations)**: Conditioned reactions and habit patterns (volitions) that get reinforced by repeated craving and aversion.
  - First seen: [Day 6 21:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yxp0mZeK2zk&t=1310s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-sankhara)
- **Sila (moral discipline)**: Ethical conduct: the foundation of the practice, supporting a calm mind and fewer regrets and agitations.
  - First seen: [Day 1 1:04:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cz7QHNvNFfA&t=3853s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-sila)
- **Vedana (sensations)**: Sensations and feeling tone. In Vipassana, observing bodily sensations is used to understand impermanence and break reactive patterns.
  - First seen: [Day 2 1:01:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cYG5VvHry7c&t=3708s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-vedana)
- **Vipassana (insight meditation)**: Insight meditation: observing reality as it is (especially impermanence of sensations) to uproot craving and aversion and develop wisdom.
  - First seen: [Day 3 0:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXXnSK2a47w&t=31s)
  - Concept Index: [jump to term](#concept-vipassana)
